Be focused from day one, the semesters are short and intense. Gradual weekly studying will save u from weeklong all nighters during finals week. Make sure to have a general idea of what u want to do, or atleast different options bc before you know it, it might be too late to have this major/class/career track. Get familiar with all the opportunities and resources, don’t be Afraid to use them! Ask Reddit if anything. Remeber your #1 priority rn is your education! Good luck.Don’t forgot your own well-being is even more important!

Set up several hours or so a week to relax/socialize. It will benefit your mental well-being.

Join the CEAS Peer Mentoring Program, you'll get paired with a mentor who will literally tell you everything there is about Stony, how to get internships, and more. They will help you along the way professionally and personally so best of both worlds.

Don’t keep a printer in ur dorm. Without it you will be forced to get out. Join SBCS. Career Center for internships and any prof with an NSF grant can request funding for an ug so after semester 1 ask your fav prof about their research opps.

Connect with your fellow students because they are you best source of info for good and bad classes and also don't be afraid to meet your major advisor because they are your best source of info for degree requirements and progress.
