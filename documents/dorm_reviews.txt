Advice on dorming:

I'm in Gender Inclusive Housing in Benedict South and it's been a positive experience for me. Most of the people in my hallway are respectful and we all interact with each other a reasonable amount. The dorms aren't incredible but I think they're pretty typical for college dorms. I would recommend it based on my experience.

On the other hand, I have heard that most other housing types/halls don't really speak to each other at all, and that some buildings have more issues than others. Mold, bugs etc., along with bad RAs and uncaring staff. It's really quite a gamble.

Best dorm out of the three: out of Kelly, tabler, and Roth

Roth because AC. And if you can get into Gershwin, no required meal plan b/c kitchen.

Should also consider Hand in Tabler quad has a kitchen too which is another chance to get out of the meal plan
Roth has AC, Tabler has big rooms, Kelly has Balconies.


Kelly is great if you want to really feel like you’re on campus if that makes sense. The dining hall is a 2 min walk but the downsides is the no ac and it gets pretty hot in there. Me and my roommate use some fans and keep the window opened at all times. Roth seems nice and more spacious inside with the same on campus feel. When I lived in west, as good as they are and everyone makes them out to be they’re so far and so secluded. Something as simple as distance affects the feeling of going to college even if you’re still on the campus. I would assume tabler might have a hint of that.
